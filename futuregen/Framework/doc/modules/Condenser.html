Condenser Module

The condenser model is a heat exchanger model that involves heat transfer between two water streams. The first is superheated water vapor (flows top to bottom on icon) that is to be condensed to liquid, and the second (flows left to right on icon) is a liquid stream used for the cooling. The heat transfer correlations used are for condensation heat transfer on tube banks.

Connections

  • Inlet Port 0 (top): Inlet water stream to be cooled.
  • Inlet Port 1 (left): Inlet water stream to serve as heat sink.
  • Outlet Port 0 (bottom): Cooled outlet water stream.
  • Outlet Port 1 (right): Sink outlet.

Graphical User Interface (UI)

This user interface prompts the user for properties of the condensing unit. These data fields are necessary in order to compute the transfer heat rate effectiveness of the unit.

The Internal Pressure Drop field allows the user to specify a desired pressure drop for the water stream as it flows through the module, whereas the External Pressure Drop specifies the change in pressure of the vapor stream through the module.
